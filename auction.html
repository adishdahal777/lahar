1972 stoneware sake set

Twelve pieces · Naoshima provenance · Estate of T. Mori

A complete twelve-piece sake service fired in 1972 at the renowned Naoshima kiln on the island of Naoshima, Kagawa Prefecture. The set includes one tokkuri (flask), six ochoko (cups), one serving carafe, two small plates, one soy dish, and one chopstick rest — each piece exhibiting the signature warm ash glaze and subtle iron speckling that defines mid-century Naoshima stoneware.

The set was part of the private collection of T. Mori, a Tokyo-based ceramic historian who spent decades documenting kiln sites across the Seto Inland Sea. Each piece shows minimal use; the original kiri-wood box (tomobako) is included, with a provenance note from the collector's estate.

This is an exceptional opportunity to acquire a complete service from one of Japan's most revered 20th-century studio pottery traditions.
