Lahar is a marketplace for considered makers — fashion, home, electronics, and the small things that take big care. We believe that buying should feel as good as making.

Our story

Lahar started around a kitchen table in Vesterbro. Two founders, frustrated with the direction of online marketplaces — the race to the bottom on price, the disposable culture, the anonymity — decided to build something different.

They wanted a platform where every product had a story, every maker was known, and every transaction felt human. Four years later, Lahar is home to 320+ independent makers across 42 countries, and we're still guided by that same principle: small batches, big care.

We don't try to compete with Amazon or Alibaba. We're not interested in infinite growth or same-day delivery. Instead, we focus on curation, craft, and connection — building a marketplace that works for the people who make and the people who buy.

What we believe

These values shape every decision we make — from how we onboard vendors to how we handle returns.

Craft over quantity

We prioritise quality, provenance, and story over volume. Every maker is vetted, every product reviewed by our curation team before it goes live. We'd rather list 10 exceptional products than 1,000 average ones.

Patience as policy

Good things take time. Made-to-order, slow shipping, thoughtful unboxing — we celebrate the wait instead of hiding from it. Every product on Lahar tells a story of hours, days, or weeks of skilled labour.

Fair for everyone

Low commissions (just 5%), transparent pricing, free returns — we built a platform that works for makers and buyers alike. No hidden fees, no algorithmic manipulation, no dark patterns.
