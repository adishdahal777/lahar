About the maker

Born in a tiny Brooklyn workshop with a soldering iron and a stubborn idea

What started in 2021 as a hobby — modding vintage headphones in a Bushwick loft — quickly became an obsession. Drew Gallagher, a former aerospace acoustics engineer, brought precision to a world he felt had gone soft: too much plastic, too many "features," not enough listen.

Today, Sundry Audio is a five-person micro-manufacturer in Brooklyn's Navy Yard. Every pair of earbuds is assembled, tested, and packed within a quarter-mile radius. Our drivers are custom-tuned on a Klippel analyzer from Germany. Our cables are braided by hand. And every product ships with a handwritten note from the person who built it.

We don't chase the annual refresh cycle. We chase the perfect seal, the natural timbre, the weight of a cable that feels like it belongs in your hand. If we haven't improved it, we don't release it. That's why we only have 14 products — and why we wouldn't trade one of them for a hundred SKUs.
