Your rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA).

Last updated: May 28, 2026

Your right to opt out

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) as amended by the CPRA, California residents have the right to opt out of the "sale" or "sharing" of their personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising. Lahar does not sell personal information in the traditional sense — we do not exchange your data for money. However, certain data-sharing practices involving advertising and analytics partners may be considered a "sale" or "share" under the broad definitions of the CCPA. We respect that distinction and provide you with the control below to exercise your rights.

Opt-out preference

Your privacy preference

Toggle this control to opt out of data sharing for advertising and analytics purposes. This preference will be stored in a first-party cookie on your device.

What happens when you opt out

Your experience remains the same

When you opt out, we will stop sharing your data with advertising partners, ad networks, and analytics providers that use your information for cross-context behavioural advertising. You will still see ads — they will just be less personalised. All core functionality of the platform remains unchanged: you can browse, purchase, message vendors, and manage your account exactly as before. Your opt-out preference is respected for 12 months, after which we will ask you to confirm it again.
