Accessibility

Making Lahar usable for everyone.

Our commitment to accessibility

Lahar is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for people of all abilities. We are actively working to conform to WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards and believe that great design is inclusive design. Accessibility is not a checklist — it is a continuous practice embedded in our design system, development workflow, and quality assurance process.

WCAG 2.1 principles

Perceivable

All non-text content has text alternatives. Videos include captions and transcripts. Content adapts to different screen sizes and orientations without loss of information.

Operable

All functionality is available via keyboard. Users have sufficient time to read content. No content flashes more than three times per second. Navigation is consistent and predictable.

Understandable

Text content is readable and uses plain language. Navigation and interactive elements behave predictably. Input fields provide clear labels and helpful error suggestions.

Robust

Our platform is built with semantic HTML and ARIA landmarks. Content is compatible with current and future assistive technologies including screen readers, switch devices, and voice control.

Ongoing efforts

What we're doing — and what we test

Accessibility is built into every phase of our product development. Our design system enforces minimum colour contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text). We test regularly with:

  • Screen readers: VoiceOver (macOS, iOS), NVDA (Windows), and ChromeVox
  • Keyboard-only navigation — full tab order, skip links, and focus indicators
  • System font scaling up to 200% without loss of functionality
  • Reduced motion, high contrast, and dark mode preferences
  • Automated accessibility audits running on every pull request via axe-core
  • Quarterly manual audits by an external accessibility consultant

Feedback

Report an issue

If you encounter any accessibility barriers on Lahar — a confusing form, an unlabeled button, or content that's hard to read — please let us know. Every report helps us improve.
